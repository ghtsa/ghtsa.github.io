Tech Education

Waxahachie Global High is one of seven schools in the state of Texas that is both an Early College High School and a STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) Academy. Global High prepares high-school students for college level work by integrating 21st century skills in all classes (e.g. - creativity, teamwork, critical thinking). Partnering with Navarro College and UT Tyler, students have the opportunity to graduate from high-school with an Associate's degree. Global High focuses on math, science, technology and engineering, which are fields of study that the average student only briefly explores.

Biology

A course that teaches students the general workings of the living world. It is generally focused on cellular composition, but also covers other areas such as organism classification.

Chemistry

A course focused on teaching students the inner workings of chemical reactions. This includes, but is not limited to, electron configuration, periodic table trends, and chemical equations.

Physics

A course provided by PLTW that teaches students basic understanding of the universe like why things move, when they move, and electromagnetic waves.

DC Biology

A course that expands on the knowledge taught in high school biology. The lessons are focused on the nature of life.

DC Chemistry

A course that expands on the knowledge taught in high school chemistry. It mainly teaches the following types of chemistry: analytical, bio, inorganic, organic, and physical.

Introduction to Engineering and Design

A course provided by PLTW that teaches students the basic skills necessary to take an engineering course or career like how to properly keep an engineering journal.

Civil Engineering

A course provided by PLTW that teaches students the basic workings housing and infrastructure. This course is vital for any students interested in becoming civil engineers.

Principles of Engineering

A course provided by PLTW that teaches students the basics of many engineering concepts like: VEX robotics, physics, and energy sources.

Robotics

A course provided by PLTW that uses VEX robotics to teach students real life application of electrons.

Computer Integrated Mechanics

A course provided by PLTW that educates students on the process of manufacturing using machinery and electronics. This is an important course that teaches skills like CAD (computer-aided design) and CAM (computer-aided design).

Digital Electronics

A course that teaches students the workings of hardware, including things like boolean algebra and logic gates.

Principles of Information Technology

A course that teaches students the beginner skills necessary to optimally use a computer, including education over different programs (like photoshop and publisher).

Networking

A course where students learn the basic communications between computers and other devices.

Computer Maintenance

A course that teaches students how to preventive and troubleshoot errors. This is important for daily life and any careers involving computers.
